## Getting Started

### Step 1: Create Your Account

1. Go to [www.aigovhub.io/register](https://www.aigovhub.io/register)
2. Enter your email and password
3. You'll be taken to your dashboard

### Step 2: Set Up Your Company Profile

This is the most important step — it tells the platform about your business so everything is personalized.

1. Go to **Dashboard > Company Profile**
2. Fill in:
   - **Industry** — Financial services, healthcare, technology, manufacturing, etc.
   - **Company size** — This affects which regulations apply to you
   - **Headquarters country** — Your primary jurisdiction
   - **Operating countries** — Every country where you do business
   - **ERP system** — SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, Dynamics 365, Workday, or other
   - **Compliance domains** — Select which areas matter: AI governance, privacy, cybersecurity, tax, ESG, etc.
3. Save your profile

**Why this matters:** The platform uses your profile to filter regulatory alerts, recommend vendors, and tailor compliance checks to your specific situation. Without it, you get generic results.

### Step 3: Choose Your Subscription

- **Free** — Blog access, AI Act checker, vendor directory, 3 compliance checks per month
- **Starter ($99/mo)** — Regulatory alerts, vendor assessments, jurisdiction tracking
- **Professional ($499/mo)** — ERP gap analysis, document generation, 25 vendor assessments
- **Enterprise ($2,000+/mo)** — Unlimited everything, API access, SSO

You can upgrade anytime at **Dashboard > Subscription** or at [www.aigovhub.io/pricing](https://www.aigovhub.io/pricing).

## Core Platform Tools

### Compliance Blog

**What it is:** An AI-powered intelligence feed that publishes 4-6 articles daily across 8 regulatory domains.

**How to use it:**

1. Go to [www.aigovhub.io/blog](https://www.aigovhub.io/blog)
2. Use the **domain filter tabs** at the top to focus on your area:
   - AI Governance, E-Invoicing, Cybersecurity, Privacy, ESG, Fintech, HR, Tax
3. Click any article to read the full analysis
4. Articles include regulatory context, affected jurisdictions, deadlines, and recommended actions

**Value delivered:** Stay informed on regulatory changes across 8 domains without reading 100-page legal documents. Each article is written by AI agents that scan 98 regulatory sources hourly.

### Regulatory Alerts

**What it is:** Personalized notifications when a regulation affects your business.

**How to use it:**

1. Make sure your **Company Profile** is filled in (country, industry, domains)
2. Go to **Dashboard > Alerts**
3. Alerts are classified by:
   - **Urgency:** Critical (action required now), High (action required soon), Medium (monitor), Low (informational)
   - **Type:** New regulation, amendment, approaching deadline, enforcement action
4. Click any alert to see the full analysis with recommended actions

**Value delivered:** Instead of manually tracking hundreds of regulations, the platform tells you exactly which ones affect your company and what to do. A single missed deadline can cost millions in fines — alerts prevent that.

**Example:** *"CRITICAL: Poland KSeF e-invoicing mandate takes effect February 2026. Your company operates in Poland. Action: Ensure your ERP supports SAF-T XML invoice submission."*

### Financial Crime & Sanctions Screening

**What it is:** Enterprise-grade entity screening against 27+ global sanctions lists.

**How to use it:**

1. Go to **SENTINEL > Screening**
2. Enter the entity to screen:
   - **Name:** Person or organization name
   - **Type:** Person, Organization, Vessel, or Aircraft
   - **Country** (optional): Narrows the search
3. Click "Screen"
4. Results include:
   - **Composite risk score** (0-100)
   - **Sanctions matches** — Which lists flagged the entity (OFAC, EU, UN, UK, etc.)
   - **PEP status** — Politically Exposed Person detection
   - **Adverse media** — Negative press and investigations
   - **Beneficial ownership** — 50% rule analysis

**Value delivered:** Sanctions screening services cost $10,000-$50,000/year for enterprise licenses. SENTINEL screens against the same lists (OFAC SDN, EU Consolidated, UN, UK HMT, Canada, Australia, and more) with fuzzy matching and AI scoring.

## CCM — Continuous Compliance Monitoring

CCM connects to your ERP system and continuously monitors transactions, access controls, and audit trails for compliance violations — replacing quarterly audits with real-time detection.

### Who Is CCM For?

- **Internal audit teams** wanting continuous monitoring instead of sample-based quarterly audits
- **SOX compliance teams** monitoring journal entries, segregation of duties, and change management
- **AML/KYC teams** screening transactions for suspicious activity
- **IT audit teams** monitoring user access and segregation of duties
- **External auditors** looking for continuous monitoring evidence

### Getting Started with CCM

1. Go to [www.aigovhub.io/ccm](https://www.aigovhub.io/ccm) and subscribe
2. An organization is created automatically with a **14-day Professional trial**
3. Follow these steps in order:

#### Step 1: Connect Your ERP

1. Go to **CCM > Connectors > New Connector**
2. Select your ERP type:
   - SAP S/4HANA Cloud, SAP S/4HANA On-Premise, SAP ECC
   - Microsoft Dynamics 365
   - Workday
   - Oracle ERP Cloud (Fusion)
   - NetSuite
3. Enter your credentials (encrypted with AES-256-GCM, never stored in plaintext):
   - **SAP:** Host URL, client ID, auth method (Basic/OAuth2/SAML/X.509)
   - **Dynamics 365:** Tenant ID, client ID, client secret, environment URL
   - **Workday:** Tenant ID, client ID, client secret
   - **Oracle:** Host URL, auth method (Basic/OAuth2), credentials
   - **NetSuite:** Account ID, consumer key/secret, token ID/secret
4. Click **"Test Connection"** — the platform will verify connectivity and report capabilities
5. Save the connector

#### Step 2: Run Your First Sync

1. Go to the connector detail page
2. Click **"Sync Now"**
3. The platform pulls data across 4 domains:
   - **SOX Controls:** Journal entries, transaction records
   - **Access Control:** User roles, permissions, SoD matrices
   - **Audit Trail:** Change documents, modification logs
   - **AML/KYC:** High-value transactions, suspicious patterns
4. Wait for the sync to complete (typically 1-5 minutes depending on data volume)

#### Step 3: Set Up Monitoring Rules

1. Go to **CCM > Rules**
2. Click **"Seed Templates"** to load built-in rules for your framework (SOX, PCI DSS, AML)
3. Or create custom rules:
   - **Threshold rules:** Alert when a value exceeds a limit (e.g., journal entries > $100,000)
   - **Pattern rules:** Alert on regex matches (e.g., round-number transactions)
   - **Missing control rules:** Alert when expected controls aren't present
   - **SoD rules:** Detect segregation of duties violations (e.g., user can both create and approve payments)
   - **Access rules:** Monitor role assignments and privilege escalation

#### Step 4: Review Findings

1. Go to **CCM > Findings**
2. Filter by severity (CRITICAL/HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW), status, framework, assignee
3. Click a finding to see:
   - **What triggered it** — The specific rule and data point
   - **AI analysis** — AI explains the risk and recommends remediation
   - **Related data** — The actual ERP records that triggered the finding
   - **Remediation plan** — AI-suggested steps to resolve the issue
4. Assign findings to team members, update status as you remediate

#### Step 5: Generate Reports

1. Go to **CCM > Reports**
2. Select report type:
   - **SOX Compliance** — Journal entry analysis, SoD violations, access reviews
   - **PCI DSS Compliance** — Payment security controls assessment
   - **AML/BSA Compliance** — Suspicious transaction analysis, screening results
   - **Access Review** — User role and permission audit
   - **Executive Summary** — High-level compliance posture for leadership
3. AI generates a narrative report with data tables, findings summary, and recommendations
4. Use these reports for: internal audit committees, external auditor evidence, regulatory examinations

---

### CCM Daily Workflow

Once set up, CCM runs continuously:

1. **Automatic syncs** pull new ERP data at your configured interval (Starter: 12h, Professional: 4h, Enterprise: 1h)
2. **Rule engine** evaluates all new data points against your monitoring rules
3. **AI analysis** automatically analyzes unanalyzed findings
4. **You review** the findings dashboard each morning and prioritize remediation
5. **Generate reports** as needed for audit committees or regulators

**Value delivered:** Traditional internal audits sample 30-60 transactions per quarter. CCM monitors *every* transaction in real-time. This means you catch issues in hours instead of months, and you have continuous evidence of compliance — not point-in-time snapshots.
